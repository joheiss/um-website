Isamu (Japanese: the brave one) is the coolest “sock” in our pack. He is so calm that hardly anything can upset him, he learns quickly and is so eager to work, that he already has a lot of tricks up his sleeve. He also has great fun searching and retrieving.


In winter, he likes to linger in front of the fireplace and in summer he enjoys relaxing on his favorite spot, a deck chair in the garden. When the sun hits his fur, it shimmers in a warm golden hue.


His impressive character prompted us to register him as a stud dog. In April 2024 he received his breeding license and is now available to suitable bitches as a stud dog.

How it all started

I've been crazy about dogs all my life! As the daughter of a hunter, I grew up with two hunting dogs. Dogs have always been an integral part of my everyday life.


In 1985 I met a Rhodesian Ridgeback for the first time - these majestic, brown dogs left a lasting impression on me.


However, it was to take a few more years before my dream of having my own Ridgeback came true. After our daughter was born, our first Ridgeback bitch “Aika Loohoowo” finally moved in with us in 1996. She was a calm and very confident representative of her breed, sometimes challenging, but we fell in love with her quirks. And so we were all infected with the “Ridgeback virus”!.

And so it went on

My parents were also so fascinated by Aika and the breed that only one and a half years later they got a half-sister of Aika. They founded their own kennel “Musambani” with her in 2001.


In 2004, we followed this example and founded our own kennel “Ushomi-Musambani”. The name “Ushomi”, which means “friend” in Swahili, and stands for the connection to my parents and their breeding line.


You can find out more about Musambani at:

www.musambani.net

Today

Our dogs are real family members for us and live with us. They accompany us almost everywhere we go - whether on vacation, shopping or to the riding stables. It is very important to us that our dogs are part of our lives.


That is why we attach great importance to strong and confident representatives of the breed.
